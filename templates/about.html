What is Faction?

The goal of Faction is to highlight economic analysis that is well-reasoned and empirically sound.

Does the minimum wage increase unemployment? To what degree has trade contributed to U.S. income inequality? How much economic damage will climate change cause this century?

These questions are tough, but largely empirical. And the amazing thing about the internet is that the best answers we have to questions like these are readily available. Sometimes the answer is "We don't know", and sometimes sifting through the debate requires time or expertise. But the analysis is out there, in blogs, op-eds, academic papers, etc.

The point of Faction is to try to get more of this analysis together in one place. You shouldn't have to spend all day on Twitter, or read dozens of blogs, to keep up with the most important economic analysis and debates.

Faction revolves around a social aggregator like Reddit or Hacker News. Users can submit or upvote articles about the economy. The shortcoming of social aggregators is that they reward popularity over quality. Over time, the hope is that Faction will include more features to help determine which analysis is most credible, by highlighting feedback from the community and letting users demonstrate their expertise.
